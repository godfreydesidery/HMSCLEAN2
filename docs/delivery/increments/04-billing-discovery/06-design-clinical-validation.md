## 2. Pay-before-service GATE â€” NET-NEW hardening, NOT legacy-faithful. Clinical recommendation: ADOPT the gate, but SCOPED by patient class.

Extraction 2/5 is correct: there is no `settled` field and no precondition check in `PatientServiceImpl`/`PharmacyServiceImpl` before order accept, result entry, or dispensing (grep zero matches). The legacy enforces pay-before-service only as a **UI filter** (`get_*_bills` lists UNPAID bills to the cashier screen) â€” never as a code gate. The prior build's "filter-not-gate" was therefore *faithful to legacy*, but the legacy behaviour is itself a clinical/financial control defect.

**Domain recommendation (this is a change-request decision, not exact-process):**
A hard gate is the clinically- and financially-correct behaviour for a Tanzanian cash/outsider workflow, but a blanket gate is clinically DANGEROUS. Scope it as follows:

- **CASH outpatient / outsider (non-insured, non-admitted):** Gate ON â€” block order *acceptance/result-release/dispensing* until the bill is PAID. This is standard Tanzanian OPD practice (pay at cashier, return with receipt). This is the population the legacy UI-filter was already trying to manage.
- **Insured (COVERED bills):** Gate effectively N/A â€” COVERED bills are `paid=amount, balance=0` at creation, so they "pass" a paid-gate automatically. Correct and safe.
- **Inpatient (admitted, VERIFIED or UNPAID):** Gate OFF for clinical services. **This is a safety blocker:** an admitted patient must NEVER be denied a lab, imaging, procedure, or medication because of an outstanding bill â€” inpatient charges settle at discharge. The `VERIFIED` status exists precisely to let inpatient orders proceed unpaid. A hard gate that blocks inpatient services on unpaid balance would be a patient-safety hazard and must be explicitly excluded.
- **EMERGENCY / not-yet-registered:** Gate OFF. Emergency stabilization cannot wait for registration or payment (MOH emergency-care obligation). Ensure the gate has an emergency-admission bypass.

> **BLOCKER-CLINICAL-2 (engagement-lead):** The pay-before-service hard gate is net-new hardening. If approved, it MUST be scoped to CASH-outpatient/outsider only, with explicit carve-outs for inpatient and emergency. A blanket "block all services until invoice PAID" gate is a patient-safety defect and must not be approved as written. Confirm scope before qa-test-engineer writes acceptance criteria.
